## Design Context

### Users
- Primary users are Deungchon Middle School staff using a PC during daily administrative work.
- The interface is a departmental link hub that helps staff jump quickly to frequently used work systems.
- Tablet and phone support is required, but those views adapt the same information architecture for secondary use rather than redefining the product.

### Brand Personality
- Clear, calm, organized.
- The interface should feel reliable for school operations, but not dry or bureaucratic.
- It should communicate polish and control rather than visual novelty for its own sake.

### Aesthetic Direction
- Desktop-first Apple-inspired light interface with a Kanban-board structure.
- Large bright canvas, restrained blue accent, soft glass navigation, rounded surfaces, and low-noise hierarchy.
- Use the uploaded school emblem as favicon and brand mark.
- Apply Paperlogy as the main typeface in the real implementation.
- Avoid dark, loud, neon, game-like, or overly dense dashboard aesthetics.

### Design Principles
- Put speed of recognition first: department navigation and link cards must be immediately scannable.
- Keep the interface bright and disciplined: one accent family, gentle shadows, soft radii, and strong spacing rhythm.
- Preserve one information model across devices: desktop uses wider board lanes, smaller screens stack the same sections vertically.
- Make interactions feel tactile but brief: sticky nav tinting on scroll and short press feedback on cards.
- Keep content editable: sample links are acceptable initially, and real links should be easy to replace later.
